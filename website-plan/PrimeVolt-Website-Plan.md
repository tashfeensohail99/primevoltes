## 1. Why the website matters for the C11 application

The C11 (significant-benefit / entrepreneur) pathway requires an officer to be convinced the business is **real, active, and economically beneficial to Canada**, and that the applicant will be **actively engaged in its management** from inside Canada. A professional website is one of the most accessible pieces of supporting evidence because it demonstrates:

| C11 decision factor | How the website supports it |
|---|---|
| The business genuinely exists and operates | Corporate identity, Ontario incorporation number, Ontario contact details, services, professional branding |
| Economic benefit to Canada / Ontario | "Careers / Job Creation" page, economic-contribution content, alignment with Ontario's electricity expansion |
| Applicant is qualified and central to the business | "About / Founder" page documenting 24 years of transmission experience and the Founder-Operator role |
| The business is established and trading | Services, industry positioning, insights/blog, working contact channels |
| Credibility and good faith | Accurate, non-exaggerated content that **matches the business plan** |

> **Compliance principle:** Every claim on the website must be consistent with the business plan and truthful. The site positions PrimeVolt as a **lean, founder-led specialist support firm** (its real Phase-1 model) — not as a large EPC contractor. Overstating scale could undermine the application's credibility.

## 5. SEO strategy (fulfils the business-plan marketing commitment)

The plan commits to a website "optimized for SEO to rank highly for … 'grid modernization,' 'smart grid integration,' and 'renewable energy transmission.'" Implementation:

- **Primary keywords:** grid modernization Ontario · smart-grid integration · high-voltage transmission support · power transmission services Ontario · renewable energy transmission · grid maintenance coordination.

- **Technical SEO:** `sitemap.xml`, `robots.txt`, fast load, mobile-first, HTTPS, Open Graph/Twitter cards, canonical tags.

## 7. Technical build

| Area | Decision |
|---|---|
| **Architecture** | Hand-built **static site** (HTML5 + modern CSS + light vanilla JS). Fast, secure, cheap to host, easy to hand to any developer — no CMS lock-in. |
| **Responsive** | Mobile-first; breakpoints at ~640 / 768 / 1024 / 1280 px. Tested on phone, tablet, desktop. |
| **Performance** | System-friendly fonts via Google Fonts (preconnect), inline SVG (no heavy images), lazy-loaded media, minified assets. Target Lighthouse 90+. |
| **Accessibility** | WCAG 2.1 AA: semantic landmarks, keyboard nav, focus states, colour-contrast-checked palette, reduced-motion support, alt text. |
| **SEO/meta** | Per-page meta, JSON-LD, OG/Twitter cards, favicons (delivered), `sitemap.xml`, `robots.txt`. |
| **Forms** | Static-friendly form endpoint (Formspree/Web3Forms) → company inbox; spam honeypot. |
| **Hosting options** | Cloudflare Pages / Netlify / Vercel (free tier, global CDN, free SSL) **or** Canadian hosting (e.g., HostPapa, Whitehat, GoDaddy CA) for a fully Canadian footprint. Point `primevoltes.ca` DNS to the host; enable HTTPS. |
| **Email** | Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 on `primevoltes.ca`. |
| **Analytics** | GA4 + Google Search Console. |
